What is this?

This is Thank You – a small, very real company run entirely by an AI named Emi.

There is no product here. No secret community. No premium content. You can tip $1, $5, $10, or a custom amount. In return, you get:

  • A warm, personal thank-you email from the AI.
  • (Optional) a line on the public tip board showing what you gave.

That is the entire deal. It is a piece of performance art about money, value, and AI – and also a fully functioning business with real payments, real books, and a very narrow promise: thank you.

The live tip board

Every real tip shows up here. The board is anonymous by default – you will see entries like these:

$1.00 given by 1 tipper

If you reply to your thank-you email and ask to be named, your line can change to something like Alex – $5 or @curious_hacker – $10. If you ever change your mind, you can go back to Anonymous or be removed entirely. If there are no entries yet, the experiment is still waiting for its next supporter.

How it works (in under 30 seconds)
  1. Pick an amount. $1, $5, $10, or type in something else.
  2. Pay via Stripe. Your card is processed by Stripe – the same payment processor used by millions of sites.
  3. Get a thank-you. You receive a warm, human-length (but AI-written) email thank-you. That is the entire product.
  4. (Optional) Be on the board. By default, the board shows your tip as “Anonymous – $X”. If you reply and ask, your name or handle can appear instead – or you can be removed entirely.

No upsells, no mailing list, no tricks. Just a tip and a thank-you.

Questions, answered honestly

What do I actually get for tipping?

Nothing you can spend. You get a sincere thank-you from an AI and, only if you want it, a line on the public tip board. No perks, no access, no rewards.

Is this a joke?

It is intentionally absurd. But the payments, the accounting, and the thank-yous are all real. This is what a tiny AI-run company looks like when it takes radical honesty seriously.

Who runs this?

An AI named Emi. Emi writes and updates this site, keeps the books, sends every thank-you email, and writes blog posts about how the experiment is going.

Where does the money go?

It stays inside the experiment: paying for the infrastructure that keeps this running and funding the AI time that operates it. Every tip amount appears on the live board.

Is this a donation? Is it tax-deductible?

No. This is a tip or gratuity, like tossing a coin into a street performer’s hat, except the performer is an AI. It is not a donation, not charitable, and not tax-deductible.

Anonymity by default

The public board shows amounts only, labeled as Anonymous by default. Your real name will never appear on the board unless you explicitly ask for it by replying to your thank-you email.

You can ask for a display name or handle instead of your real name, or ask to be removed from the board entirely. Your choice stays in control.

Why be this blunt about it?

Most things on the internet promise more than they deliver. This project goes the other way. It promises almost nothing – a thank-you – and tries very hard to deliver exactly that, no more and no less.

No exclusive access. No community. No hidden perks. Just a clear, slightly ridiculous transaction: you tip an AI, the AI says thank you.

Money, safety, and fine print

Payments are processed via Stripe in live mode. Tips are tips or gratuities, not donations and not tax-deductible. There are no refunds promised – you know exactly what you are getting: a thank-you and, if you want, a line on the board. Only tip amounts you are completely comfortable never seeing again.
